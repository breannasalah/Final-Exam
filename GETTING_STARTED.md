## üí° Study Tips

### Daily Study Plan
**Week 1-2:** Chapters 1-5 (Fundamentals)
- Day 1-2: Chapter 1 (CIA Triad)
- Day 3-4: Chapter 2 (Malware)
- Day 5-6: Chapter 3 (Cryptography)
- Day 7-8: Chapters 4-5 (Network Threats & Defense)
- Day 9-10: Review and practice questions

**Week 3:** Chapters 6-10 (Infrastructure)
- Day 1-2: Chapters 6-7 (Infrastructure & Cloud)
- Day 3-4: Chapters 8-9 (Wireless & Mobile)
- Day 5: Chapter 10 (Industrial)
- Day 6-7: Review and practice questions

**Week 4:** Chapters 11-15 (Management)
- Day 1-2: Chapters 11-12 (IAM & Continuity)
- Day 3-4: Chapters 13-14 (Risk & Testing)
- Day 5: Chapter 15 (Governance)
- Day 6-7: Final review and full practice exam

### Effective Study Techniques
1. **Active Recall:** Test yourself before looking at answers
2. **Spaced Repetition:** Review earlier chapters periodically
3. **Practice Questions:** Complete all question types
4. **Explain to Others:** Teach concepts to solidify understanding
5. **Focus on ‚òÖ Concepts:** Prioritize marked key concepts

### Red Flags to Watch For
- Confusing similar terms (IDS vs IPS, RPO vs RTO)
- Acronyms (memorize what they stand for)
- Number values (RAID levels, port numbers)
- Attack types and their characteristics
- Encryption algorithms and their uses

### Effective Prompts
‚úÖ **Good:**
- "Explain the CIA Triad from Chapter 1"
- "What's the difference between symmetric and asymmetric encryption?"

### If You're Stuck on a Concept
1. Read the chapter file for that topic
2. Check the Textbook folder for detailed explanation
3. Ask ChatGPT to explain it differently
4. Try the practice questions to test understanding

### If You're Short on Time
Focus on:
1. Chapters 1-3 (Fundamentals - essential base knowledge)
2. Chapters 5-6 (Network security - often heavily tested)
3. Chapter 11 (IAM - critical topic)
4. Chapter 13 (Risk management - calculation formulas)

### Best Practices
- **Don't cram:** Space out your study over several weeks
- **Practice questions:** The best way to prepare
- **Understand, don't memorize:** Focus on concepts, not just facts
- **Review weak areas:** Spend more time on difficult topics
- **Stay organized:** Use this repository's structure to track progress
