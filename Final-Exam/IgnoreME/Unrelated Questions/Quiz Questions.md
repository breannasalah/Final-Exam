# **Module 1 – Introduction to Security (pp. 3–32)**

  

**Question 2**

  

Which threat actors violate computer security for personal gain?

  

A) Red hat hackers

B) White hat hackers

C) Black hat hackers

D) Gray hat hackers

  

**Answer: C) Black hat hackers**

**Explanation:** Black hat hackers violate security for malicious or personal financial gain. Their motivation is explicitly to cause harm or commit crime.

**Why students miss this:** Gray hats _sometimes_ break rules, which confuses people, but gray hats do not act for personal gain—black hats do.

Which type of threat actor would benefit the most from accessing your enterprise’s new machine-learning algorithm research and development program?

  

A) Competitors

B) Brokers

C) Criminal syndicates

D) Shadow IT

  

**Answer: A) Competitors**

**Explanation:** Competitors would seek intellectual property or proprietary algorithms to gain market advantage.

**Why students miss this:** “Brokers” can also steal data, but brokers _sell_ vulnerabilities—they do not benefit from intellectual-property R&D.

**Question 10**

  

Which standardized framework was developed by NIST to help organizations assess and manage risks and integrate cybersecurity?

  

A) CIS Controls

B) ISO 27001

C) Risk Management Framework (RMF)

D) Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

  

**Answer: C) Risk Management Framework (RMF)**

**Explanation:** RMF (NIST SP 800-37) is a structured, lifecycle-based process used to integrate security and privacy into information-system risk management.

**Why students miss this:** CSF _also_ relates to risk, but CSF is voluntary and high-level; RMF is the formal, prescriptive NIST risk-assessment process.
